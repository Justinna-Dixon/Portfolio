About Me

Justinna Dixon, PhD, RN is a health services researcher and nurse scientist with expertise in clinician wellbeing, mental health policy, and advanced quantitative methods. She is currently a postdoctoral fellow at the Center for Health Outcomes and Policy Research (CHOPR) and the National Clinician Scholars Program at the University of Pennsylvania. She earned her PhD in Nursing from Columbia University and her Bachelor's of Science in Nursing from Rutgers University- New Brunswick. She is a first-generation college student and a proud alumna of the Rutgers School of Nursing Educational Opportunity Fund Program.
